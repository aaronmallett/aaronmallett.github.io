Our Process

Unlike other treatments, our process allows for a lot of flexibility. Since most of our patients require 2 or 3 consecutive sessions, it is extremely common for someone to fly out, have the procedure and fly back over just a few days. We select patients based on their medical history and imaging studies, so there’s no need to fly in for a consultation or exam before your procedure. See a more detailed overview of our process below.

Before:

Medical Review

Contact us to discuss your situation. After reviewing your detailed medical history and imaging studies, we’ll find out if MUA is right for you.

Consultation

Next, we’ll set up a consultation either in person or over the phone. Since your medical records will be the deciding factor, we only have to meet for a consultation if you want to.

Anesthesia Clearance

After we have decided that MUA is right for you, you’ll need to see a medical doctor to be cleared for anesthesia.

Preparation:

Your Arrival

No food or drinks for at least 6 hours prior to the procedure (medication should still be taken with just a sip of water). Be sure to have a “designated driver” who can take you home after the procedure.

Forms & Exams

After filling out the intake forms, a nurse will do a quick exam and put your IV in. Your MUA provider will be in to do another brief exam.

Anesthesia

Next, you’ll be taken to the OR and your Anesthesiologist will do a quick consult and administer your anesthesia.

Procedure:

Manipulation

The procedure will be performed by a certified MUA doctor while you are under minor doses of anesthesia. This will take approximately 15 minutes, depending on the amount of work needed.

Waking Up

You will be taken to the recovery room, where a nurse will monitor you as you wake up. This typically takes 20-30 minutes.

Follow Up

You will be released with instructions for home care, such as icing and stretching. The next day, you will be re-examined to see if you need any additional procedures.

Outcomes:

Immediate Relief

Some patients are shocked to report experiencing immediate and significant improvement following their MUA procedure.

Recovery Period

Some patients may be quite sore immediately following the procedure, but they will still report an improvement in their overall range of motion and optimism right away.

No Improvement

While it is extremely rare, sometimes MUA cannot relieve your pain. This can occur in patients with severely damaged structures. Our highly selective qualification process helps to ensure that this outcome doesn't happen often.

Manipulation Under Anesthesia ("MUA") is a multi-specialty approach to treating chronic musculo-skeletal pain. A licensed physician and a licensed chiropractor are both involved in the overall procedure – the physician is solely responsible for determining the medical appropriateness and ordering, as well as administering of anesthesia while the chiropractor is solely responsible for performing the necessary manipulations while the patient is "asleep." Patients should be aware that there is no direct or indirect financial relationship between the physician and chiropractor who are involved in the MUA procedure. The physician is a Board-certified anesthesiologist and the chiropractor is a licensed practitioner who has received specialized training in how to perform MUA. Klass Chiropractic, P.C. does not guarantee or warrant that patients will actually ‘wake up pain free.’ This is simply a marketing phrase to convey to potential patients who are appropriately screened and treated through MUA that they are reasonably likely to see a reduction in their pain over time.
