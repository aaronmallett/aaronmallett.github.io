Do I Qualify?

MUA is an extremely effective tool for chronic pain relief, and most of those who qualify experience improved range of motion, improved outlook and the ability to continue rehabilitation to help the scar tissues heal. To see if you're a good candidate for MUA, answer this short questionnaire. If you answer yes to all of these questions, MUA may be right for you. Contact us at 1.844.MUA.DOCS or email address for more information.

MUA isn't for everyone. To see if you pre-qualify, answer these four questions.

Is your condition one that is known to respond well to conservative and manipulative therapy?

See examples

Herniated disc or degenerative disc disease without fragmentation with or without radiculopathy. Neck and/or back pain. Facet syndrome. Scar tissue/adhesive contractures of the neck or back. Myofascial pain syndrome Acute and chronic muscle pain and contracture/spasm. Decreased spinal range of motion. Arthritis. Cervicobrachial syndrome. Cervicogenic headaches. Failed back surgery. Functional biomechanical dysfunction syndromes.

Do you have imaging studies demonstrating one or more of the conditions above?

Have you attempted pain medication, physical therapy, injections, chiropractic, massage or even surgery with little to no results?

Does your condition cause you disability, such as preventing or limiting you from working or performing daily or recreational activities?

If you answered yes to all of these questions, then MUA may be right for you. Contact Dr. Klass at 1.844.MUA.DOCS to find out more.

Manipulation Under Anesthesia ("MUA") is a multi-specialty approach to treating chronic musculo-skeletal pain. A licensed physician and a licensed chiropractor are both involved in the overall procedure – the physician is solely responsible for determining the medical appropriateness and ordering, as well as administering of anesthesia while the chiropractor is solely responsible for performing the necessary manipulations while the patient is "asleep." Patients should be aware that there is no direct or indirect financial relationship between the physician and chiropractor who are involved in the MUA procedure. The physician is a Board-certified anesthesiologist and the chiropractor is a licensed practitioner who has received specialized training in how to perform MUA. Klass Chiropractic, P.C. does not guarantee or warrant that patients will actually ‘wake up pain free.’ This is simply a marketing phrase to convey to potential patients who are appropriately screened and treated through MUA that they are reasonably likely to see a reduction in their pain over time.
